Anomaly Scan



18 - 20 weeks

Where to go


The "Anomaly scan" is with the Ultrasound technician, you should book this through your midwife. If you don't have a midwife, please find the nearest GP to get one.

What to expect


The purpose of this scan is to check the physical development of your baby and make sure that there are no physical abnormalities.

The anomaly scan is carried out in the same way as the dating scan, with gel on your tummy and the sonographer passing the ultrasound device backwards and forwards over your skin.

You will be offered a scan that produces a two-dimensional (2-D) black and white image that gives only a side view of the baby. The NHS screening programme does not use 3-D or colour images.

The anomaly scan looks for some physical abnormalities in the baby. The scan only looks for these problems and cannot find everything that might be wrong.

The anomaly scan is offered to everybody, but you do not have to have it if you do not want to. The scan is a medical examination. You will be asked to give your permission for it to be carried out. Make sure you understand what is going to happen, and feel free to ask any questions.
